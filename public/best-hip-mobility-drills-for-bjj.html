Best Hip Mobility Drills for BJJ (Ranked by ROM Impact)

There are hundreds of hip drills online, but only a handful move the needle for BJJ. This ranked list cuts through the noise: these are the drills that build the deep flexion and rotation your guard demands, ordered by how much usable range they actually deliver.

Here are the highest-impact hip mobility drills for grappling, ranked.

How Are These Drills Ranked?

By usable range-of-motion impact for BJJ — how much each one improves the flexion and rotation you rely on in guard, and how well the range sticks. Active, loaded drills rank highest because the range they build lasts.

RankDrillPrimary range
190/90 hip switches (+ lift-offs)Internal + external rotation
2PAILs/RAILs on frog stretchExternal rotation, adductors

Why Is 90/90 Number One?

The 90/90 position trains internal and external rotation together, which is the exact combination guard retention needs. Add active lift-offs (raising the trailing leg) and it builds strength at end range too — not just passive range.

Which Drills Build Range That Lasts?

The loaded and active ones: 90/90 lift-offs, PAILs/RAILs, and Cossack squats. Passive stretches feel good but fade fast. If you only have ten minutes, spend it on active work.

How Do You Fit These Into Training?

  • Warm-up: hip CARs and 90/90 switches, dynamic and short.
  • After class or rest days: PAILs/RAILs, Cossack squats, deep squat pry with longer holds.
  • Daily: a quick CARs circuit to maintain what you build.

Key Takeaways

  • Only a handful of hip drills meaningfully help BJJ.
  • 90/90 switches with lift-offs rank first for combined rotation.
  • Active, loaded drills build range that lasts; passive ones fade.
  • Use dynamic drills before class and loaded work after.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the single best hip drill for BJJ?

The 90/90 hip switch, especially with active lift-offs. It trains the internal and external rotation guard retention needs.

How many hip drills should I do per session?

Two to four focused drills beat a long random list. Quality and consistency win.

Do passive stretches help at all?

They give short-term range and can prep a muscle, but active loaded work is what makes range stick.

How long until these drills improve my game?

Most grapplers feel a difference in guard within 4 to 8 weeks of consistent work.
