BJJ Belt Ranks Explained: White to Black

The adult BJJ belt system runs white, blue, purple, brown, then black, with four stripes marking progress within each belt. Unlike some martial arts, BJJ belts are famously hard to earn — the journey from white to black typically takes about a decade of consistent training.

Here is what each belt means and how the system works.

What Are the BJJ Belt Colors in Order?

For adults, the main progression is:

  1. White — every beginner starts here.
  2. Blue — solid fundamentals and a broad base of technique.
  3. Purple — advanced technique and a developing personal game.
  4. Brown — refinement and consistency, close to mastery.
  5. Black — expert-level skill and deep understanding.

Beyond black are degrees and, eventually, the coral and red belts reserved for decades of contribution.

What Do the Stripes Mean?

Each colored belt can hold up to four stripes, awarded by your instructor to mark progress between belt promotions. Four stripes on a belt generally signals you're approaching the next color, though timing is always at the instructor's discretion.

How Is Each Belt Earned?

There is no universal test. Promotions are awarded by your instructor based on skill, mat time, consistency, understanding, and often how you perform in rolling and competition. Different academies weigh these differently, which is part of why timelines vary so much.

These are typical ranges, not rules — individual paths vary widely.

Are Kids' Belts Different?

Yes. Youth BJJ uses additional colors (white, grey, yellow, orange, green and their variations) before students transition into the adult system, usually around age 16.

Key Takeaways

  • Adult belts go white, blue, purple, brown, black, with up to four stripes each.
  • Stripes mark progress between belt promotions.
  • Promotions are instructor-awarded on skill and mat time, not a fixed test.
  • Kids use extra belt colors before joining the adult system around 16.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the BJJ belts in order?

For adults: white, blue, purple, brown, and black, followed by degrees and the coral and red belts for lifetime contribution.

How many stripes are on a BJJ belt?

Up to four per colored belt, awarded by your instructor to mark progress toward the next belt.

Is there a test to get promoted in BJJ?

Usually no formal universal test. Instructors promote based on skill, consistency, mat time, and performance.

What belt is a beginner in BJJ?

White belt. Everyone starts there regardless of background in other martial arts.
