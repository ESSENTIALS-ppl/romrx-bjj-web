How to Know If You're Flexible Enough for BJJ (Self-Test)

You do not need to be a gymnast to do BJJ, but a few key ranges make everything easier and safer. This self-test walks you through simple checks for your hips, shoulders, and spine so you can see exactly where you stand — and what to work on first.

Grab a spot on the mat or floor and run through these.

Do You Need to Be Flexible to Start BJJ?

No. You can start BJJ at any flexibility level. But limited range in a few key areas will make guard harder, escapes slower, and certain positions uncomfortable. The goal is not extreme flexibility — it's enough functional range to move freely.

Hip Self-Tests

  • Deep squat hold: Can you sit in a flat-foot squat, chest up, for 30 seconds? Struggling points to limited flexion and ankle range.
  • Butterfly sit: Soles together, tall spine — do your knees drop toward the floor? High knees mean limited external rotation.
  • 90/90 switch: Rotate side to side smoothly between both positions to check internal and external rotation.

Shoulder Self-Tests

  • Overhead reach: Raise both arms straight overhead without your lower back arching. Cheating with the back means limited shoulder range.
  • Behind-the-back clasp: One hand over the shoulder, one up the back — can your fingers meet? Compare both sides for imbalance.

Spine and Ankle Self-Tests

  • Cat-cow range: Can you round and arch your spine segment by segment? Stiffness here shows up in stacking and inversions.
  • Knee-to-wall: Knee toward the wall, heel down — how far back can your foot go? Limited dorsiflexion caps your deep squat and guard.

What Do Your Results Mean?

Any test that feels blocked is your priority. You do not need to pass all of them to train — use the failures as your personal mobility to-do list, and retest every few weeks to track progress.

Key Takeaways

  • You can start BJJ at any flexibility level.
  • A few key ranges in hips, shoulders, spine, and ankles make it easier and safer.
  • Use the self-tests to find your weakest links.
  • Turn failed tests into your mobility to-do list and retest regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be flexible to start BJJ?

No. You can start at any level. Mobility just makes guard, escapes, and certain positions easier over time.

How flexible should a BJJ player be?

Enough functional range in the hips, shoulders, spine, and ankles to move freely. Extreme flexibility is not required.

How often should I retest?

Every 3 to 4 weeks. It's a good way to see whether your mobility work is paying off.

What if I fail most of the tests?

That's just your starting point. Prioritize the tightest areas and improve them one at a time.
